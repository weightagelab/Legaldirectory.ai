Property Registration
Documents & Procedure
Everything you need to know about registering property in India documents, process, and common questions answered.
Documents Required (Tentative List)
#Document / StepDetails
01Property Value EstimationAccurate estimate required for stamp duty calculation per the applicable ready reckoner.
02Sale DeedPrimary document transferring ownership. Must be executed and signed by all parties.
03Stamp Duty & Registration ChargesVaries by state. Deficit stamp duty results in registration refusal. Women buyers may receive rebates.
04Sub-Registrar ApproachApproach the Sub-Registrar of Assurances in whose jurisdiction the property is situated.
05Document SubmissionSubmit originals with property card and proof of stamp duty payment before the registrar.
06Identity DocumentsAadhaar, PAN, or government-issued ID for all parties and two witnesses. Biometrics will be scanned.
Registration FAQ
Who must be present during registration? +
Authorised signatories for both seller and purchaser must be present with two witnesses. All must carry valid photo ID. If a Power of Attorney is used, it must be presented. Biometric identity of all parties including witnesses will be scanned at the registrar's office.
What if a company is party to the agreement? +
The representative must carry a Power of Attorney or Letter of Authority along with a Board Resolution copy authorising them to complete the registration on the company's behalf.
What is stamp duty and how is it calculated? +
Stamp duty is the tax paid to the government for legal ownership of an asset. It varies by state and is calculated based on the property's market value or ready reckoner rate (whichever is higher). Women buyers often receive rebates in most states.
What happens if stamp duty paid is insufficient? +
The sub-registrar verifies stamp duty against the ready reckoner before registration. If there is any deficit, registration will be refused. Our lawyers ensure the correct amount is calculated before you appear at the office.
What is the role of witnesses? +
Two witnesses are mandatory for registration. They must present valid ID proofs and address proofs before the sub-registrar, and their biometric identity will also be scanned. Without proper witnesses, registration cannot be completed.
Report & Will FAQ
Is it mandatory to register a Will? +
No. Registration of a Will is not mandatory under Indian law. However, registering it with the Sub-Registrar under the Indian Registration Act, 1908 significantly reduces the chance of it being challenged after the maker's death and is strongly advisable.
Can a Will be video recorded? +
Yes. Video recording of the Will-making process is admissible as evidence under the Indian Evidence Act, 1872. It serves as a strong safeguard against future allegations of fraud or execution under duress.
Can a Muslim distribute property through a Will? +
Under Muslim personal law applicable in India, a Muslim may dispose of only up to one-third of their property by Will (after funeral expenses and debts) without the consent of heirs. This is governed by personal law, not the Indian Succession Act, 1925.
What does the Property Search Report include? +
The report covers ownership chain continuity, pending financial liabilities or loans, missing or invalid documents, legal red flags, compliance with local regulations, and any encumbrances or disputes. It is prepared after a 200–300 page review and includes a dedicated post-delivery consultation.
What is the difference between Agreement to Sale and Sale Deed? +
An Agreement to Sale (ATS) is a commitment to transfer property at a future date upon fulfillment of conditions it does not immediately transfer ownership. A Sale Deed is the final document that actually transfers ownership and must be registered at the Sub-Registrar's office to be legally effective.
